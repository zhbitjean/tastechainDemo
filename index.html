1. Problem statement – why TasteChain exists

The global restaurant industry runs on thin margins (5%–10%) and fragile supply chains. Most operators don’t have standardized data, centralized systems, or access to AI inventory and purchasing tools. Developers, meanwhile, lack a live, permissioned restaurant data network to build serious AI products on.

TasteChain is created to solve four core pain points:

  • Opaque & unstable supply chains – volatile prices, high waste, inconsistent suppliers.
  • No usable data for AI – scattered POS logs, manual spreadsheets, or no data at all.
  • Developers can’t access real industry data – no shared dataset, no standard API, no licensing channel.
  • Ultra-thin restaurant margins – any improvement in procurement, inventory, or cost control is valuable.
What restaurants need
  • Better purchasing and inventory turnover
  • Transparent supplier and price comparisons
  • AI “co-pilot” for daily operations, not just reports
  • A low-friction way to share and monetize data

TasteChain positions real restaurant data as an asset class, wrapped in AI and Web3 rails.

2. TasteChain solution – three layers

TasteChain is a decentralized restaurant industry data & AI platform, built from three tightly connected layers.

2.1 Data Network Layer

Live data streams from cooperating restaurants:

  • Purchasing data (daily/weekly inbound goods)
  • Inventory (ingredients, consumables, packaging)
  • Dish sales and demand trends
  • Raw material cost & price volatility
  • Staff scheduling & labor patterns
  • Logistics and delivery performance
  • Supplier quotes and contracts
  • Energy & equipment usage (optional sensors)

Data is cleaned via the TasteChain SDK, stored in an off-chain secure data lake, and hashed on-chain to guarantee integrity and provenance.

2.2 AI Intelligence Layer

Industry-grade AI models provide predictions and optimization:

  • Inventory forecasting (Stock Predictor)
  • Ingredient waste & spoilage prediction
  • Hot dish trends & demand analysis
  • Purchasing strategy optimization
  • Multi-supplier price comparison (Market Matcher)
  • Staff scheduling optimization
  • Back-of-house consumable anomaly alerts
  • Store profit and loss analytics

All models are exposed via APIs for restaurant dashboards and third-party apps.

2.3 Developer Platform Layer

TasteChain provides an open platform for developers to build restaurant applications on top of standardized data and AI:

  • Standard APIs (inventory, sales, supply-chain, AI forecasts)
  • Sandbox data + developer SDKs
  • Smart contract templates
  • Plugin / App marketplace
  • Restaurant-side control panel & data permission system

Developers can build ordering systems, CRM & loyalty, delivery tools, kitchen management, supply-chain auction markets, staffing tools, and AI recommendation engines. The platform charges 5%–10% fees; the rest flows to developers.

3. Technical architecture

TasteChain is organized into five technical layers, from data ingestion to applications:

3.1 Data Ingestion Layer
  • POS system integrations
  • Handheld scanners & mobile apps
  • Inventory sensors (optional)
  • Supplier APIs
  • Manual input modules for small operators
3.2 Secure Data Lake
  • Data cleaning & normalization
  • De-identification & masking
  • Encrypted storage
  • Multi-level access permissions
3.3 Blockchain Layer
  • On-chain hashes of data batches
  • Audit trails for data usage
  • Smart contracts for apps, settlement & tokens
  • TASTE token economy logic
3.4 AI Compute Layer
  • Model training & fine-tuning
  • Model deployment & scaling
  • API-based inference serving
  • Real-time or scheduled predictions
3.5 Application Layer
  • Restaurant dashboards
  • Developer dashboards
  • API explorer & documentation
  • Plugin / App marketplace
